Risk Management Fundamentals

01 What Is Risk Management?
Risk Management is the process of identifying, analyzing, and controlling possible threats that can harm information systems. A risk happens when a threat can take advantage of a weakness.

Simple Formula:
Risk = Threat + Vulnerability + Impact
A weak password is a vulnerability. A hacker is a threat. Account takeover is the possible impact.
02 Common Security Risks
Organizations may face different types of security risks:
• Data breach
• Malware infection
• Phishing attack
• Weak passwords
• Unauthorized access
An employee receives a phishing email (threat), clicks a malicious link (vulnerability), and the attacker gains access to internal systems (impact).
03 Steps in Risk Management
Risk management usually follows these steps:

1. Identify the Risk — find possible threats and weaknesses.

2. Analyze the Risk — understand how likely the risk is and how serious the damage can be.

3. Evaluate the Risk — decide which risks need immediate attention.

4. Treat the Risk — apply controls to reduce or remove the risk.

5. Monitor the Risk — continue checking if the risk still exists or becomes worse.
A school identifies that its online enrollment system could go down during peak periods. They analyze the likelihood as high and impact as severe, so they implement load balancing and backup servers.
04 Risk Treatment Options
After identifying risks, organizations can choose how to handle them:

Avoid the Risk — stop the activity that causes the risk.

Reduce the Risk — apply security controls to lower the risk.

Transfer the Risk — move the risk to another party, such as insurance or service providers.

Accept the Risk — allow the risk if the impact is low or the cost of fixing it is too high.
A school may reduce the risk of data loss by creating regular backups. They may accept the low risk of a rarely-used legacy system.
05 Risk Management Example
Scenario: A school uses an online enrollment system.

Possible risks:
• Students cannot access the system during enrollment.
• Unauthorized users may access student data.

Possible solutions:
• Create backups
• Use user roles
• Monitor system activity
