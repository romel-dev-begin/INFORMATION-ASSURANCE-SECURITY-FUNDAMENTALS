Principles of Information Assurance

01 What Is Information Assurance?
Information Assurance is the process of protecting information and making sure it remains safe, correct, reliable, and available. It is not only about preventing hackers. It also includes proper data handling, system monitoring, backup, recovery, policies, and user responsibility.

Simple Meaning: Information Assurance makes sure that the right information is accessed by the right people at the right time.
A student portal uses Information Assurance to protect grades, personal information, class schedules, and payment records by requiring login, restricting teacher access to their own records, and preventing unauthorized grade changes.
IA ensures the right information is accessed by the right people at the right time.
02 Main Purpose of Information Assurance
The main purpose is to reduce risks and protect information from possible threats. It helps organizations:
• Protect sensitive data
• Prevent unauthorized access
• Maintain accurate records
• Keep systems working properly
An organization implements multi-factor authentication, regular backups, and access logs to reduce the risk of data breaches.
IA is a holistic approach — technology, people, and processes working together.
03 Core Principles
Information Assurance is guided by several important principles:

Confidentiality — information should only be accessed by authorized users.

Integrity — information should remain accurate and unchanged unless properly updated.

Availability — information and systems should be accessible when needed.

Authentication — verifies the identity of a user.

Authorization — controls what an authenticated user is allowed to do.

Non-repudiation — ensures that a person cannot deny an action they performed.
A bank uses PIN + biometrics (authentication), transaction limits (authorization), and encrypted logs (non-repudiation) to secure accounts.
These six principles form the foundation of all security frameworks.
