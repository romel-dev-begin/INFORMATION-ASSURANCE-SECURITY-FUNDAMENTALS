TOPIC-02 MONITORING

Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability

01 What Is the CIA Triad?
The CIA Triad s a basic model in information security that explains the three main goals of protecting information. CIA stands for:
Confidentiality — protecting information from unauthorized access.

Integrity — keeping information accurate, complete, and trustworthy.

Availability — information and systems must be accessible when needed.
A hospital system uses the CIA Triad to protect patient records: only doctors can view them (Confidentiality), records cannot be altered without authorization (Integrity), and the system is available 24/7 for emergencies (Availability).
02 Confidentiality
Confidentiality means protecting information from unauthorized access. Only people with permission should be able to view sensitive data.

Common controls include passwords, encryption, and access control.
Students can only view their own grades. Employees can only access files related to their work.
03 Integrity
Integrity means keeping information accurate, complete, and trustworthy. Data should not be changed by unauthorized users or accidentally modified.

Common controls include audit logs, file validation, and access permissions.
A student grade should not be changed without teacher approval. A report should not be edited by someone without permission.
04 Availability
Availability means information and systems must be accessible when needed. Even if data is private and accurate, it is useless if users cannot access it.

Common controls include backups, disaster recovery plans, and system maintenance.
A school portal should be available during enrollment. A hospital system should be accessible during emergencies.
